Why are Carbon Emissions a Problem?

In 2019, the City of Melbourne declared a climate and biodiversity emergency, joining an international movement and recognising that climate change posed a serious threat to the people of Melbourne and Australia. The climate in Melbourne has already warmed by 1.0°C, and the emergency acknowledges that a further 0.5°C increase in climate temperature would lead to major irreversible damage done to ecosystems. Melbourne is already experiencing many extreme changes due to climate change, such as hotter days, flooding, intense storms, bushfires, and more.

Further Consequences

If this continues, Melbourne will continue to face increasingly severe climate issues and changes just like the ones we are already facing. If this is allowed to occur, it could prove disastrous towards our people, ecosystems, animals, economy, and more. So, it is incredibly important for us to act fast, so that we can hit our carbon goals by 2050 to prevent any further damage to the climate.
