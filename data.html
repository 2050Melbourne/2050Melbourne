Reach NET Zero by 2050

The Victorian government aims for half of new car sales to be electric vehicles by 2030, but we have not yet joined the long list of countries aiming to do fully ban the sale of old fashion fuelled cars. Such countries include the UK, the US, China and multiple countries in the EU(weforum). Such a change will ensure that a large majority of vehicles will be electric by 2050. On a side note, this should come with the establishment of multiple ev charging stations throughout the city.

Livestock

Animals livestock are great contributors to carbon emissions of the greenhouse type. If we manage the livestock better by feeding them more efficiently, breeding them to make less carbon emitting animals, and handling their waste better, we can reduce methane emissions from livestock. Crop production also produces carbon emissions. If we perfect agricultural techniques, lower chemical involvement, and rotate crop rotations, we can lower crop carbon emissions. Soil is a contributor to carbon emissions, and to combat this we can put to use strategies such as reduced tillage, cover cropping, and organic farming. Being more efficient with our energy will additionally help with lowering carbon emissions. Switching to renewable energy types for agricultural activities will reduce carbon emissions from energy use. Wasting food and water is another carbon emission contributor. By lowering our food and water wasting rates, we can greatly reduce carbon emissions created by food production.

Gas to Electricity

Currently, Victoria supports some electrification projects but still relies on natural gas. To reduce these manufacturing emissions, we can take a similar approach. The government can reduce the amount of money they tax companies if they change their manufacturing processes to run on 100% electricity. We can also start to force companies to shift from traditional heating methods to hydrogen heating methods, providing subsidies so help speed up the process. And also pass a law that all new industrial building must run on electricity

Financial Incentives

Offer financial incentives for people to switch their homes from gas to electric. As long as there are no real benefits for people to switch their homes from gas to electric. It will cost money and time. But if the government offered a large sum of money that would cover the cost of the switch and reward homeowners, we will most definitely see a rise in electric homes. And we must make induction cooktops the default option when building a new home, while also making electric heating mandatory for new homes. We can also offer saving on bulk purchases on electric appliances.
